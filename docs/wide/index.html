Predicate: wide

Characterizing Axioms

To be wide is to be in the Hi region of a width scale. The chief inferences one can defeasibly draw from something being in the Hi region of a scale are distributional – e.g., wider than average – and functional – wide enough or too wide for some purpose.

(forall (x)
   (iff (wide x)
        (exists (s s0 s1)
           (and (widthScale s s1 e) (Hi s0 s) (inScale x s0)))))

Additional Axioms

Secondary shape/size terms, such as “plump”, “skinny”, “slender”, and “slim”, can often be partially characterized in terms of basic adjectives.

(forall (x)
   (if (plump x)
       (and (wide x) (thick x))))
