Predicate: or'

Summary

(or' e e1 e2)

e is the eventuality of either e1 or e2 existing.

Characterizing Axioms

Disjunction is defined analogous to conjunction: If the eventuality e is the disjunction of eventualities e1 and e2, then e really exists exactly when one of e1 and e2 really exists.

(forall (e e1 e2)
  (if (or' e e1 e2)
      (and (eventuality e) (eventuality e1) (eventuality e2)
           (iff (Rexist e)
                (or (Rexist e1) (Rexist e2))))))

The likelihood of the disjunction e of two eventualities e1 and e2 is greater than or equal to the likelihood of either of them.

(forall (s e e1 e2 d d1 d2 c)
  (if (and (likelihoodScale s) (likelihood d1 e1 c) (likelihood d2 e2 c)
           (or' e e1 e2) (likelihood d e c))
      (and (leqs d1 d s) (leqs d2 d s))))
