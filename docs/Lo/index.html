Predicate: Lo

Summary

(Lo s1 s)

s1 is the low region of scale s.

Characterizing Axioms

We can construct composite scales out of existing scales.

(forall (s1 s) (if (Lo s1 s) (subscale s1 s)))

The bottom of a scale is the bottom of its Lo region.

(forall (s1 s)
   (if (Lo s1 s)
       (forall (x)
         (iff (bottom x s) (bottom x s1)))))

If a point is in the Lo region, then it is less than all the points in the Hi region.

(forall (s s1 s2 x y)
   (if (and (Hi s1 s) (Lo s2 s) (inScale x s1) (inScale y s2))
       (lts y x s)))

   (forall (x z)
      (iff (near x z)
           (exists (s s1)
              (and (nearnessScale s z) (Lo s1 s) (onScale x s1)))))
