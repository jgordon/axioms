Predicate: imply

Summary

(imply' e e1 e2)

e is the eventuality of an implicational relation between e1 and e2 existing.

Characterizing Axioms

(forall (e e1 e2)
  (if (imply' e e1 e2)
      (and (eventuality e) (eventuality e2)
           (or (eventuality e1) (eventualities e1)))))

(forall (e e1 e2)
  (if (imply' e e1 e2)
      (iff (Rexist e)
           (if (Rexist e1) (Rexist e2)))))

Additional Axioms

(exists (e1 e2)
  (and (imply e1 e2) (not (gen e1 e2))))

(forall (e e1 e2)
  (if (and (imply' e e1 e2) (Rexist e) (Rexist e1))
      (Rexist e2)))

(forall (e1 e2 e3 i1 i2 i3)
  (if (and (imply' i1 e1 e2) (imply' i2 e2 e3) (imply' i3 e1 e3)
           (Rexist i1) (Rexist i2))
      (Rexist i3)))

(forall (s1 s2)
  (iff (inconsistent s1 s2)
       (and (eventualities s1) (eventualities s2)
            (exists (e1 e2)
              (and (imply s1 e1) (imply s2 e2) (not' e2 e1))))))

(forall (s1 e2)
  (iff (minimallyProves s1 e2)
       (and (not (member e2 s1))
            (imply s1 e2)
            (not (exists (s2)
                   (and (properSubset s2 s1)
                        (imply s2 e2)))))))

We will take a conditional event to be the implicational relation between some eventuality – a state or an event – and an event, with a further relation that the first eventuality must obtain at the beginning of the second.

(forall (e e1 e2)
  (iff (cond e e1 e2)
       (and (imply' e e1 e2) (event e2)
            (forall (t)
              (if (begins t e2)
                  (atTime e1 t))))))

The principal property of positive modalities is that Modus Ponens can be applied within the modality.

(forall (e1 e2 m)
  (if (and (PosMod e1 m) (imply e1 e2))
      (PosMod e2 m)))

An eventuality is possible iff the constraints do not imply a negation of the eventuality.

(forall (e c)
  (iff (possible e c)
       (forall (e1)
         (if (not' e1 e)
             (not (imply c e1))))))

An eventuality e is necessary with respect to a set of constraints c iff c implies e.

(forall (e c)
  (iff (necessary e c)
       (imply c e)))

If the likelihood of an eventuality e is d, we can say that e is at d on the likelihood scale, if c holds.

(forall (d e c s e1)
  (if (and (likelihood d e c) (likelihoodScale s) (at' e1 e d s))
      (imply c e1)))
