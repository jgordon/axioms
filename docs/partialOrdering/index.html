Predicate: partialOrdering

Summary

(partialOrdering e x y s)

e is a partial ordering on the components of s, where x is less than y.

Characterizing Axioms

(forall (e x y s)
   (if (partialOrdering e x y s)
       (and (scale s) (arg* x e) (arg* y e))))

Additional Axioms

(forall (s)

            (exists (s1 e x y s2 s3)

(forall (e x1 y1 s)

The predicate before is a partial ordering on the elements of a temporal sequence.

(forall (e t1 t2 s)
  (if (and (tseq s) (before' e t1 t2))
      (partialOrdering e t1 t2 s)))

Essentially, x is more functional than y if x is higher on the functionality scale, where being in the Hi region of the functionality scale means something is functional (which means there is an event involving it that brings about its functionality).

(forall (e f s x y)
  (if (and (partialOrdering e x y s) (functionScale s f))
      (lessFct' e x y f)))
